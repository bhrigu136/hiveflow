Privacy

Privacy Policy

Effective from May 16, 2026

What we collect and why

When you sign up for HiveFlow, we ask for your name, email, and a password. That's it. We need your name so your teammates know who they're working with, your email so you can log in and reset your password if you forget it, and your password so nobody else can get into your account. We hash your password before storing it — we literally cannot see what it is even if we tried.

If you upload a profile picture, we keep it on our servers. If you create tasks, projects, or discussions, that data lives in our database so it's there when you come back. Pretty standard stuff.

Google Calendar — how that works

You can optionally hook up your Google Calendar. When you do, Google gives us a couple of tokens that let us create, edit, or remove calendar events on your behalf. We only touch your calendar — nothing else in your Google account. If that makes you uncomfortable, just don't connect it, or disconnect it any time from your dashboard. No hard feelings.

We follow Google's rules on handling user data, including the Limited Use requirements they set out for apps that use their APIs.

Who sees your data

Short answer: nobody outside of HiveFlow. We don't sell your information. We don't hand it over to advertisers. We don't run analytics trackers that follow you around the internet. The only people who see your tasks and projects are you and the teammates you've invited into your organizations.

How we keep things secure

Your data sits in a PostgreSQL database hosted on Supabase's infrastructure (which runs on AWS). Every connection between your browser and our servers is encrypted with TLS. Your session cookie is marked HttpOnly and Secure, meaning it only travels over HTTPS and can't be read by JavaScript running on the page. Passwords are hashed with PBKDF2 — an industry-standard algorithm that makes brute-forcing impractical.

Could we do more? Always. Security is something we keep working on, not a box we check once.

Cookies

We use exactly one cookie: the session cookie that keeps you logged in. That's it. No tracking pixels, no third-party analytics cookies, no advertising nonsense. Just the one cookie doing its job.

What you can do about your data

You can view and edit your name, email, and profile picture from the Profile page whenever you want. You can delete any tasks, projects, or discussions you've created. You can disconnect Google Calendar. If you want your entire account deleted, drop us an email and we'll take care of it.

Emails we send

We'll only ever email you for two reasons: to verify your email when you sign up, and to send you a password reset code if you request one. That's it. No newsletters, no promotional emails, no "we miss you" guilt trips.

Updates to this policy

If we change something meaningful here, we'll put a notice in the app. We're not going to silently rewrite things and hope nobody notices.

Questions?

If anything here is unclear or you want to know more, reach out at tamanna900620@gmail.com. Happy to explain.
